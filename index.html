A. About Me

Good day, everyone. I'll make this introduction brief, as I've already done it...what, twice now? So, I'm going to focus on everything that's happened thus far instead of re-stating my name, age, and personal information.

First things first, I'm exhausted. I'm not even sugarcoating it at this point. These past few weeks have been physically and mentally draining. I haven't had a proper break in who-knows-how-long. Despite this, I'm still going. I still wake up every morning. I still face the day. No matter how hard things get, I will persist. I have a life to live. I have people who need me. I have a purpose I must fulfill.



Second, my hyperfixations have evolved. My interest in object shows has been thriving, suprisingly. Since BFDI episodes have been consistently releasing from late 2024-onwards, I'm getting my daily dose of silly little objects competing in reality shows. I've also grown to love other shows such as The Nightly Manor, ONE, Paper Puppets Take 2, etc. For Roblox, on the other hand, I can never seem to escape the Dead-by-Daylight inspired games. I haven't been able to play, but I've tried out Forsaken and absolutely fell in love with the characters. There's PIZZA MAN, dude! How could I NOT love Elliot?

I'm still struggling with my writing. I haven't been able to write any proper works due to the sheer amount of schoolwork, but I've managed to finally develop the "Telorania Cinematic Universe," as they call it (the name's unofficial, please don't hunt me, Marvel). See, I've been making worlds, characters, and storylines for the past few years since the Pandemic, and they've grown suprisingly well with time. I've been watching story writing commentaries for a while, and they've been helping make my characters and settings feel more natural, human, if I will.

And finally, we transition into the actual portfolio. This was admittedly pretty rushed, but here we are, getting it done on time (hopefully)! If you've seen our to-do lists, you would know all of Grade 9 is currently tweaking because of the piling requirements. I'm literally cramming as we speak. Soooooooooooooooo, without further ado, here's the full TLE-ICT E-Portfolio!

G. Reflection

I'll be frank here. I did not enjoy JavaScript. I was definitely more optimistic last quarter due to the success that was CSS, but I've realized I don't really like technical-heavy things all too well. Sure, I may be a news writer for Standard Press, but at least I knew how it worked and had sufficient experience for it. I jumped into JavaScript completely blind and never got the chance to bring out my full potential on it, especially with how hectic things have been, and, honestly, I don't think I want to. I knew I was never really one for all this internet-coding stuff anyways.

I wished I could've done better, with more than just ICT. I know I'm enough, but I know I can do so much more if I just tried hard enough.
